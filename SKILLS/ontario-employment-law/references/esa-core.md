# Ontario ESA Core Rules — Wages, Overtime, Vacation, Termination

## Source: Employment Standards Act, 2000, S.O. 2000, c. 41
Always verify current rates at: https://www.ontario.ca/document/your-guide-employment-standards-act-0

---

## 1. Minimum Wage

| Category | Rate (2025-2026) |
|---|---|
| General minimum wage | $17.20/hour |
| Student minimum wage (under 18, under 28hrs/week or school holidays) | $16.20/hour |
| Homeworkers | $18.90/hour |

ESA Rule: If no rate was agreed upon, minimum wage applies.
If an agreed rate was below minimum wage, minimum wage applies anyway.

## 2. Hours of Work
- Maximum hours per week: 48 hours (unless employee agrees in writing to work more)
- Overtime threshold: 44 hours/week
- Daily rest: 11 consecutive hours off per day
- Weekly rest: 24 consecutive hours off per week

## 3. Overtime Pay
- Overtime applies after 44 hours in a work week
- Rate: 1.5x the employee's regular rate
- Overtime is per week, not per day

Calculation:
Regular pay = hours up to 44 x regular rate
Overtime pay = hours over 44 x (regular rate x 1.5)

## 4. Vacation Pay

| Years of Employment | Vacation Entitlement | Vacation Pay Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Less than 5 years | 2 weeks | 4% of gross wages |
| 5+ years | 3 weeks | 6% of gross wages |

Vacation pay is owed on ALL wages including overtime. Calculated as % of TOTAL gross earnings.

## 5. Public Holiday Pay
Ontario has 9 public holidays per year.
Public holiday pay = (wages in the 4 weeks before the holiday) divided by 20
Employee must work their last scheduled day before and first scheduled day after the holiday.

## 6. Termination Pay

| Length of Employment | Minimum Notice or Pay in Lieu |
|---|---|
| Less than 3 months | No entitlement |
| 3 months to 1 year | 1 week |
| 1-3 years | 2 weeks |
| 3-4 years | 3 weeks |
| 4-5 years | 4 weeks |
| 5-6 years | 5 weeks |
| 6-7 years | 6 weeks |
| 7-8 years | 7 weeks |
| 8+ years | 8 weeks |

ESA termination pay: Only applies if employed for 3+ continuous months.
Severance pay (separate): Applies only if employed 5+ years AND employer has payroll over $2.5M.

With cause termination: Employer claims no notice required. However:
- Cause must be proven, not assumed
- Allegations of misconduct do NOT automatically constitute cause
- Even if cause existed, ESA minimum notice may still be owed

## 7. Wage Statements
Employers must provide a written wage statement each pay period showing:
pay period dates, hours worked, rate of pay, gross wages, deductions, net wages.
Failure to provide wage statements = ESA violation.

## 8. Record-Keeping
Employers must keep employment records for each employee for 3 years.
If an employer claims they have no records, this works in the employee's favour.
The employer bears the burden of proving compliance.

## 9. Reprisals (Retaliation)
An employer cannot penalize or threaten an employee for:
- Asking about their ESA rights
- Filing an ESA claim
- Participating in an ESA investigation
Reprisal = separate ESA violation.

## 10. Common Employer Defences and How to Counter Them

| Employer Defence | Counter-Argument |
|---|---|
| No written contract | ESA applies regardless of written agreement |
| They were a contractor | ESA presumes employee status; employer must prove contractor |
| Payments were goodwill | Regular payments for work = wages regardless of label |
| There was cause | Cause must be proven; allegations are not proof |
| They agreed to these terms | ESA minimums cannot be waived or contracted below |
| It was trial/experimental | Trial periods are still employment; ESA applies from day 1 |
