# Evidence Standards — What You Need & How to Present It

## Core Principle
Every claim must be tied to evidence. The stronger and more specific your evidence,
the stronger your case. Where evidence is missing, label it clearly and explain your methodology for estimates.

---

## Evidence Hierarchy (Strongest to Weakest)

| Tier | Evidence Type | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 — Direct Proof | Original documents with timestamps | Bank statements, email with headers, exported WhatsApp, contracts |
| Tier 2 — Strong Corroboration | Secondary documents | Receipts, photos with metadata, calendar exports, GPS records |
| Tier 3 — Supporting Evidence | Witness statements, reconstructed logs | Work logs, witness declarations, location history |
| Tier 4 — Circumstantial | Indirect evidence | Consistent payment patterns, Canada Job Bank wage data |

---

## Evidence Needed for Each Claim Type

### Unpaid Wages
| Evidence | Purpose | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Bank / e-transfer records showing payments received | Proves what was paid | CRITICAL |
| Reconstructed work log (dates, hours, location) | Proves hours worked | CRITICAL |
| Any messages confirming schedule or tasks | Corroborates hours | Strong |
| Agreed rate of pay (message, email, verbal note) | Anchors rate claim | Strong |
| Canada Job Bank / industry wage data | Supports minimum wage argument | Useful |

### Overtime
| Evidence | Purpose | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Work log showing hours per week | Proves overtime threshold crossed | CRITICAL |
| Messages or timesheets showing work hours | Corroborates hours | Strong |

### Reimbursements / Expenses
| Evidence | Purpose | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Original receipts (gas, materials, tools) | Proves expense amount | CRITICAL |
| Messages pre-approving the expense | Proves authorization | CRITICAL |
| Messages assigning you to a task requiring the expense | Links expense to work | Strong |
| Mileage log | Supports mileage claim | Strong |

### Employee Status
| Evidence | Purpose | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Messages assigning tasks, giving instructions | Proves control (employee factor) | CRITICAL |
| Evidence of working exclusively for employer | Proves exclusivity | Strong |
| Evidence of employer providing tools/materials | Proves tools factor | Strong |
| Employer's own use of word "employment" | Employer admission | Strong |

### Termination
| Evidence | Purpose | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Termination notice itself | Proves termination date | CRITICAL |
| Start date evidence | Proves length of employment | CRITICAL |
| No evidence of cause provided at time of termination | Weakens "cause" defence | Strong |

## How to Collect Evidence Properly

WhatsApp / iMessage / SMS:
- Best: Export the full chat (WhatsApp: Chat > More > Export Chat > With Media)
- Good: Screenshot with timestamps visible
- Avoid: Cropped screenshots with no context

Emails:
- Best: Export as .eml or PDF with full headers
- Good: Forward to yourself (headers preserved)
- Avoid: Screenshots of emails (metadata lost)

Bank Records:
- Best: Official PDF statement downloaded from bank portal
- Good: Screenshot of e-transfer history with dates and amounts
- Avoid: Verbal description of payments without documentation

Photos / Videos:
- Best: Original file with metadata (timestamp + GPS embedded)
- Note: Check that location services were enabled when photos were taken

Receipts:
- Best: Original paper receipt or digital receipt email
- Good: Clear photo of receipt with all details visible

## Integrity Rules

1. Never alter original evidence — make copies before anything else
2. Hash all files — use SHA-256 to prove integrity (Python script in your case folder)
3. Label everything — file names should identify what the document is
4. Keep originals — give copies to Ministry/court, not originals
5. Organize by ID — use EV-001, EV-002 etc. and reference them consistently
6. Note chain of custody — record where and when you obtained each item

---

## Reconstructed Evidence (When Originals Are Missing)

When you cannot produce original records:
1. Be transparent — label it "reconstructed" or "best estimate"
2. Show your methodology — explain how you calculated the number
3. Corroborate with other evidence — even indirect corroboration helps
4. Reference the employer's obligation — employers must keep records; their failure to produce records hurts them, not you

---

## Red Flags — Evidence Problems to Fix Early

| Problem | Impact | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Only screenshots of messages (no export) | Weaker authenticity | Get full export |
| No record of hours worked | Cannot prove wage amount | Reconstruct now |
| No bank records | Cannot prove what was paid | Download statements |
| Expenses without receipts | Reimbursement claim weakened | Gather what you have |
| Only verbal account of pay agreement | Employer will deny | Find any corroborating message |
