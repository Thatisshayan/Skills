# Ontario ESA Claim Process — Step by Step

## Filing Portal
https://www.ontario.ca/page/file-employment-standards-claim

## Overview
The Ministry of Labour, Immigration, Training and Skills Development (MLITSD) enforces the ESA.
Filing an ESA claim is:
- Free — no filing fees
- No lawyer required — you can represent yourself
- Handled by an Employment Standards Officer (ESO)
- The ESO investigates and can order the employer to pay

---

## Step 1 — Prepare Before You File

About the Employer:
- Employer's full legal name (not just trade name)
- Employer's business address, phone, email
- CRA Business Number (if known)
- Type of business

About Your Employment:
- Your job title / role
- Start date and end date of employment
- Regular work schedule (days and hours)
- Rate of pay (agreed or minimum wage)
- How you were paid (cash, e-transfer, cheque)
- Total hours worked (best estimate with calculation)

About Your Claim:
- What violations occurred (unpaid wages, overtime, vacation, termination pay, etc.)
- Amount you believe you are owed for each violation
- Dates of each violation
- Supporting documents (communications, bank records, receipts, timesheets)

---

## Step 2 — File the Claim Online

1. Go to: https://www.ontario.ca/page/file-employment-standards-claim
2. Click "Start your claim"
3. Log in or create an Ontario account
4. Complete all sections of the form
5. Attach supporting documents (PDF format preferred)
6. Review everything before submitting
7. Submit and save your confirmation number immediately

Tips:
- Be specific and factual — avoid emotional language
- State amounts clearly with your calculation shown
- If you don't know an exact amount, give your best estimate and explain your methodology
- Attach as much evidence as you have — more is better

---

## Step 3 — After Filing

What Happens Next:
1. Your claim is assigned to an Employment Standards Officer (ESO)
2. The ESO contacts you to discuss your claim (can take several weeks)
3. The ESO contacts the employer for their response
4. The ESO may request additional documents from both parties
5. The ESO investigates and makes a determination

Your Obligations After Filing:
- Respond promptly to ESO requests
- Provide any additional documents requested
- Keep records of all communications with the ESO
- Do not contact the employer directly about the claim

---

## Step 4 — Possible Outcomes

| Outcome | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Order to Pay | ESO finds in your favour and orders employer to pay |
| No Violation Found | ESO finds no ESA violation occurred |
| Settlement | You and employer agree to a resolution during investigation |
| Referral to OLRB | Complex cases may go to the Ontario Labour Relations Board |

If an Order to Pay is Issued:
- Employer must pay within the time specified
- If employer doesn't pay, the Ministry can enforce the order
- You can also register the order as a court judgment

---

## Step 5 — If You Disagree with the Decision

You can object to an Employment Standards Officer's decision within 30 days.
The objection goes to the Ontario Labour Relations Board (OLRB) for review.

---

## Key Rules About Filing

Limitation Period: 2 years from the date of the last ESA violation.
Recovery Period: The Ministry can recover wages for up to 2 years before the claim date.
File as early as possible to maximize recovery.

ESA vs. Civil Court:
- Once you accept an ESO's order to pay, you generally cannot also sue in civil court for the same amount.
- Filing an ESA claim does NOT prevent you from also filing a common law wrongful dismissal claim.
- Choose your strategy carefully — consult a lawyer/paralegal before deciding.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Waiting too long (limitation period issue)
- Being vague about amounts or dates
- Not attaching supporting documents
- Contacting the employer during the investigation
- Accepting a lowball settlement without understanding your full entitlement
- Withdrawing a claim prematurely
